# F2 — E2.2 kernel microbenchmark results

Date: 2026-04-30. Branch: `exp/v0.2-validation`.

## Question

What is the per-frame `cv::remap` cost at 4K BGR8 on each target
host, with a representative fisheye + radial-tangential LUT, and
how much headroom does it have above Prism's 10 Hz target rate?

E2.1 closed the GPU comparand. This is a CPU absolute-baseline
measurement only.

## Method

`bench/experiments/scripts/cv_remap_4k_bench.py`. Standalone
Python harness, no ROS, no GStreamer. cv2.remap on a random
4K BGR8 frame with a LUT computed from baked-in intrinsics
(K: fx=fy=1900, principal at image center; D: −0.30, 0.10, 0,
0, −0.02 — significant radial distortion). 1000 iterations,
first 100 dropped as warmup, p50/p95/p99 reported.

Same script runs on both hosts (cv2 4.13 on Intel, cv2 4.8 on
Orin); both versions ship the same SIMD-optimized C++ remap
kernel and the timing is the kernel time, not the
Python/numpy boundary.

## Results

| Host          | p50 ms  | p95 ms  | p99 ms  | fps ceiling at p50 | headroom over 10 Hz |
| ---           | ---     | ---     | ---     | ---                | ---                 |
| Intel desktop | 3.824   | 4.397   | 5.326   | 261.5              | 26×                 |
| Orin Nano     | 63.393  | 64.787  | 66.532  | 15.8               | 1.5×                |

## Implication for F2

**Intel desktop**: kernel cost is small relative to ROS plumbing.
The F2 win on this host will be dominated by DDS round-trip
elimination, the same v0.1.0 mechanism that produced the resize
and crop wins. Predicted E2.3 numbers: Prism ~5–8 ms, stock
~12–15 ms, ~50 % delta — same shape as the v0.1.0 anchor.

**Orin Nano**: kernel cost dominates. cv::remap at 4K is 63 ms
on a single Cortex-A78AE core, vs DDS round-trip cost on the
order of 5 ms. F2 on Orin at 4K therefore has:

- a predicted ~7 % delta (~5 ms saved on a ~67 ms total) —
  positive, but small,
- only 1.5× headroom over the 10 Hz target — uncomfortable for
  production duty,
- no usable hardware fallback (E2.1 confirmed nvvidconv has no
  warp).

This mirrors the v0.1.0 colorconvert structural finding: on Orin
at 4K, the kernel is the ceiling, not Prism's plumbing. The
honest published characterization will need to flag this per host
and per resolution.

At lower resolutions the picture shifts. cv::remap is roughly
linear in pixel count; predicting Orin at 1080p:

- kernel ~16 ms (4× pixel ratio)
- DDS round-trip ~5 ms
- predicted delta ~25 %, comfortable headroom over 10 Hz.

That makes F2 deployable on Orin at typical robotics resolutions
(1080p or below) but marginal at 4K. The v0.2 RectifyNode README
and site rows will need the per-resolution characterization, not
just per-host.

## Decision for E2.3

Proceed. The ladder has produced enough information to predict
E2.3's shape on both hosts; E2.3's role is now to validate the
prediction with a real ROS A/B and produce the v0.2 numbers.

E2.3 captures: 4K @ 10 Hz on both hosts (matching v0.1.0's
anchor methodology) plus an additional 1080p capture on Orin
to surface the resolution-sensitive story.
