# F2 — E2.3 end-to-end A/B results (Intel desktop, partial)

Date: 2026-04-30. Branch: `exp/v0.2-validation`. Commits:
prototype `cb6a99f`, harness `910c313`.

## Question

Does `prism::RectifyNode` (CPU-only, direct mode, cv::remap from
runtime CameraInfo LUT) beat `image_proc::RectifyNode` end-to-end
on the segment Prism targets, by virtue of intra-process composition
and DDS round-trip elimination?

## Method

Same 120 s capture shape as v0.1.0. Both sides launched in their
own `component_container` from `launch/A_B_comparison.launch.py`,
new `operation:=rectify` graph. Source: `prism::MediaStreamerNode`
publishing 4K (or 1080p) BGR8 at 10 Hz with a baked-in
fisheye + radial-tangential CameraInfo (focal_x/y=1900, plumb_bob
D=[-0.30, 0.10, 0, 0, -0.02], same intrinsics as the E2.2 kernel
microbench so end-to-end numbers stay comparable to the standalone
baseline). Latency = `recv_ns - msg.header.stamp_ns` at the same
Python rclpy CaptureNode subscribing to both sides.

## Results — Intel desktop

### 4K source (matches v0.1.0 anchor methodology)

| Side | n | p50 ms | p95 ms | p99 ms | mean ms | fps |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Prism | 1199 | 73.75 | 160.38 | 245.28 | 82.24 | 9.99 |
| Stock | 1200 | 71.64 | 160.07 | 262.43 | 79.86 | 10.00 |

**Δ p50: +2.9% (Prism is 2.11 ms slower than stock).** Statistical
noise.

### 1080p source (resolution-shifted)

| Side | n | p50 ms | p95 ms | p99 ms | mean ms | fps |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Prism | 1200 | 24.88 | 41.31 | 50.93 | 25.83 | 10.00 |
| Stock | 1200 | 25.10 | 39.56 | 48.94 | 25.86 | 10.00 |

**Δ p50: −0.9% (Prism wins by 0.22 ms).** Statistical noise.

## Why F2's predicted DDS-elimination floor does not materialize

Three reasons, ranked by contribution:

1. **Rectify preserves frame size.** Output is the same 4K (or
   1080p) BGR8 image as the input. The CaptureNode's Python rclpy
   receive path runs at ~70-80 ms per 4K frame and ~10 ms per 1080p
   frame. Both stock and Prism push frames through that same
   bottleneck on egress; whatever differences exist on the
   processor side get absorbed.

2. **image_transport in Humble may already intra-process inside a
   composable container.** Stock side uses
   `image_transport::CameraSubscriber`; if image_transport's intra-
   process path is active when the publisher and subscriber are in
   the same container, the source → rectify hop on stock has the
   same intra-process zero-copy as Prism. The v0.1.0 win mechanism
   (DDS round-trip on the source → processor hop, present on
   stock, absent on Prism) is not present here.

3. **The cv::remap kernel is identical on both sides.** Both
   implementations ultimately call OpenCV's SIMD-optimized remap.
   Since the kernel is the same and the path overhead is the same,
   the measured latency is the same.

This is the v0.1.0 4K colorconvert structural finding in a
different dressing: when the receive path becomes the ceiling, the
upstream architecture difference is masked.

## Implication for F2

**F2 as a standalone rectify wrapper is a wash vs
`image_proc::RectifyNode` on Prism's segment.** The CPU-only
intra-process design is correct (it leaves GPU free for perception
consumers, the operator's compartmentalization principle), but the
end-to-end latency claim that v0.1.0's DDS-elimination floor would
provide does not show up for this operation shape.

Where F2 *would* show up:

- **Chain-mode rectify** (rectify + resize + colorconvert in a
  single `prism::ImageProcNode`, vs three image_proc nodes piped
  through DDS). Same architectural mechanism as v0.1.0's chain
  row. Would shrink the per-frame output to a small frame and
  remove two DDS hops between the three stock nodes.
- **Embedded SoCs with weak Python rclpy receivers** (RubikPi,
  RK3588) where the receive path is the bottleneck would behave
  differently again — both sides queue, structural finding
  identical.

## Next-step decision

Three honest options for the operator:

- **F2A — Pivot to chain-mode rectify.** Add a `rectify,resize`
  comparison vs `image_proc::RectifyNode → image_proc::ResizeNode`
  piped through DDS. This is a v0.1.0-mechanism story (one DDS
  hop on Prism vs two intermediate hops on stock) applied to the
  rectify-inclusive case. Predict ~50% delta on the chain.
- **F2B — Ship F2 as a wash but reframed.** Standalone rectify
  with an honest "tie on latency, win on architectural fit"
  framing. The CPU-only-by-design line is the only pillar that
  remains.
- **F2C — Drop F2.** No measurable end-to-end win on the standalone
  case, no clear demand signal that the chain-mode case (F2A) is
  what users want. Reactivate when one of: (a) RK3588 / RubikPi
  capture host enters scope, (b) a chain-mode rectify benchmark
  proves the v0.1.0 mechanism still works through a rectify stage.

My read: F2A is the cleanest path. It restores the v0.1.0 mechanism
to the rectify story (single-pipeline architecture beats
DDS-piped multi-node graph), produces a published number, and
matches how rectify is actually used downstream (rarely as the
*final* op — usually feeding into a resize or colorconvert before
ML inference).

## Honesty note

The 4K 73 ms / 71 ms numbers and 1080p 25 ms / 25 ms numbers are
real. Publishing them as a Prism win would be dishonest; they are
a Prism wash. The F2A pivot is not changing the data — it is
changing what gets compared to surface a different architectural
mechanism.
